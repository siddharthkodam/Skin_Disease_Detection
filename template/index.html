What are Skin Diseases?

Skin diseases are conditions that affect your skin. These diseases may cause rashes, inflammation, itchiness or other skin changes. Some skin conditions may be genetic, while lifestyle factors may cause others. Skin disease treatment may include medications, creams or ointments, or lifestyle changes.

Skin Type and Skin Diseases

Acne

Acne is a common skin condition characterized by the presence of pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads. It usually occurs during puberty but can affect people of all ages.

Bullous Disease

Bullous disease is a group of disorders causing fluid-filled blisters on the skin or mucous membranes. It can be due to autoimmune conditions, genetics, medications, infections, or underlying medical issues.

Rosacea

Rosacea is a chronic skin condition characterized by facial redness, flushing, visible blood vessels, and sometimes, acne-like breakouts. It primarily affects the central part of the face, including the cheeks, nose, forehead, and chin.

Cellulitis

Cellulitis is a bacterial infection that affects deeper skin layers, causing redness and swelling. It requires antibiotics for treatment.

Impetigo

Impetigo is a contagious bacterial infection characterized by red sores or blisters with a honey-colored crust. It can be treated with antibiotics and good hygiene practices.

Bacterial infections

Bacterial infections are caused by harmful bacteria invading the body. They can affect different parts of the body and require antibiotics for treatment. Prompt medical attention is essential for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Eczema, or atopic dermatitis, is a chronic skin condition causing dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. Treatment includes moisturizers, topical corticosteroids, and avoiding triggers. Preventive measures involve keeping the skin moisturized, using mild products, and managing stress. Consult a healthcare professional for personalized care.

warts

Warts are a type of skin infection caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV). The infection causes rough, skin-colored bumps to form on the skin. The virus is contagious. You can get warts from touching someone who has them.

Viral Infections

Viral infections are caused by viruses invading the body. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms, rest, and supportive care. Prevention involves good hygiene, handwashing, vaccination, avoiding close contact with sick individuals, safe food handling, and mosquito control. Consult a healthcare professional for accurate diagnosis and guidance.
